Quadruple Chocolate Cake

Description

This cake is not named for the bypass you might feel you'd need after eating it, but in honour of the four choc-factors that comprise its glory:

Cocoa to make the cake; chocolate chips or morsels to fold into it; a chocolate syrup to drench it once out of the oven; flakily sliced dark chocolate to top it before slicing.

Ingredients

For the Cake

  • 200 grams plain flour
  • ½ teaspoon bicarbonate of soda
  • 50 grams cocoa powder
  • 275 grams caster sugar
  • 175 grams soft unsalted butter
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 80 millilitres sour cream
  • 125 millilitres boiling water
  • 175 grams dark chocolate chips (unless you prefer milk)

For the Syrup

  • 1 teaspoon cocoa powder
  • 125 millilitres water
  • 100 grams caster sugar
  • 25 grams dark chocolate

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to gas mark 3/170°C/150°C Fan/325ºF. Line tin with greased foil, baking paper or use silicone tin.

  2. Put the flour, bicarbonate of soda, cocoa, sugar, butter, eggs, vanilla and sour cream into a bowl and mix until smooth.

  3. Scrape down with a rubber spatula and process again while pouring the boiling water down into mix slowly.

  4. Stir in chocholate chips/pieces.

  5. Pour the mix into your loaf tin and cook for 1 hour. When it's ready, the loaf will be risen and split down the middle. Test with a skewer; it will come out clean-ish if the cake is ready.

  6. When the cake has baked for 45 - 50 minutues, put the syrup ingredients of cocoa, water and sugar into a small saucepan and boil for 5 minutes until dark and syrupy.

  7. Take the cake out of the oven and sit it on a cooling rack. Pour the syrup over the cake evenly.

  8. Let the cake become completely cold and then remove from tin. Sprinkle with chocolate.
