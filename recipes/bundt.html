Lemon Bundt Cake

The Cake:

Rather than a loaf, this lemon drizzle comes in the form of a bundt. It’s packed with refreshing lemon flavour — perfect to serve at a summertime picnic.

Ingredients

  • 250g unsalted butter, softened
  • 250g plain flour
  • 1½ tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp fine salt
  • 225g caster sugar
  • Finely grated zest 3 lemons
  • 4 large free-eggs, at room temperature

For the Syrup

  • 100g caster sugar
  • Juice of 3 lemons

For the Icing Drizzle

  • 80g icing sugar

Method

  1. Heat the oven to 180°C/fan160°C/gas 4. Generously butter the inside of the bundt tin using your hands.

  2. Sift the flour, baking powder, and salt into a large bowl.

  3. In another large bowl, using an electric whisk, cream together the butter, sugar and lemon zest until the mixture is very pale and fluffy.

  4. Add the eggs one at a time, whisking well after each addition, then (using a metal spoon) fold in the flour mixture, taking care not to knock out the air you’ve so carefully whisked in.

  5. Dollop the mixture into the greased bundt tin and smooth the top, then bake for 35-40 min or until a skewer inserted into the centre comes out clean.

  6. While the cake is baking, make the syrup: heat the sugar and lemon juice in a pan and bubble for 3 min until reduced by half, then transfer to a shallow bowl to cool completely.

  7. When the cake is cooked, remove from the oven, poke holes all over the surface and, while the cake is still warm, pour over the cooled syrup.

    Leave the syrup to soak into the cake for 15 min, then turn the bundt out of the tin onto a wire rack and leave to cool completely.

  8. For the topping, put the icing sugar into a jug and add a tsp cold water. Sir well with a fork until smooth. Drizzle over the cake, leave to set for 15 min, then cut into slices and serve.
